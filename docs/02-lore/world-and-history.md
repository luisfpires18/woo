# World & History

> The lore backbone of Weapons of Order. Every gameplay feature involving lore must be cross-referenced with this document.

---

## The World: Aethermoor

The world of **Aethermoor** is a fractured continent surrounded by treacherous seas, dense jungles, and towering mountain ranges. It was once a unified empire — the **Aetherial Dominion** — ruled by a council of wise forgemasters who kept balance through the craft of magical weapons.

That era ended with the **Sundering**.

---

## The Ancient Era: The Aetherial Dominion

Thousands of years ago, the people of Aethermoor discovered that certain metals, when forged with rune-inscribed techniques, could channel the world's latent magical energy — called **Aether**. The greatest forgemasters formed the **Aetherial Council**, a governing body that unified the continent under a shared purpose: to harness Aether responsibly.

The Council created the **Great Forge** — a colossal structure at the heart of the continent — and through it crafted seven legendary weapons of unmatched power. These weapons were needed to seal breaches in the **Veil**, a metaphysical barrier separating Aethermoor from the **Abyss**, a realm of entropy and chaos.

The seven weapons succeeded. The Veil was sealed. Peace reigned for centuries.

But power corrupts.

---

## The Corruption: Birth of the Weapons of Chaos

Over generations, the Council grew divided. Some members sought to use the weapons not to protect, but to dominate. A faction within the Council — led by the forgemaster **Morath** — secretly modified the seven weapons, infusing them with Abyssal energy drawn from the very breaches they had sealed.

The modification worked. The weapons became **far more powerful** — but they were tainted. They whispered to their wielders, fed them ambition, and slowly eroded their sanity. These became the **Weapons of Chaos**:

1. **Voidfang** — A sword that cuts through any defense, but its wielder sees enemies in every ally.
2. **The Tide Ender** — A trident that commands the seas, but slowly drowns the wielder's memories.
3. **Ashbringer's Maw** — A war axe that sets everything aflame, but the fire eventually consumes the wielder's lands.
4. **The Iron Veil** — A shield that renders its bearers invincible, but turns them to living statues over time.
5. **Whisper of Thorns** — A bow that never misses, but each kill takes a piece of the wielder's soul.
6. **Gravelord's Fist** — A war hammer that shatters mountains, but each strike ages the wielder by years.
7. **The Entropy Staff** — A staff that warps reality, but the caster slowly loses the ability to distinguish real from unreal.

---

## The Sundering

When the corruption was discovered, civil war erupted. The Council fractured. Morath and his followers tried to seize all seven weapons but failed. In the final battle — the **Sundering** — the Great Forge was destroyed, and the continent was torn apart by the unleashed Abyssal energy.

The aftermath:
- The unified Dominion collapsed.
- Three surviving civilizations retreated to the regions they could defend:
  - **Veridor** — to the coasts and islands
  - **Sylvara** — into the deep jungles and ancient forests
  - **Arkazia** — to the mountains and highland fortresses
- The seven Weapons of Chaos were scattered across the broken continent, each coming to rest in a **Chaos Shrine** — places where the Veil had thinned.
- Morath disappeared. His followers went underground.

---

## The Current Era: The Age of Fragments

Centuries have passed. The three kingdoms have rebuilt, each developing distinct cultures, military traditions, and relationships with the remnants of Aetherial forge-craft.

The Weapons of Chaos remain in their shrines, guarded by the Abyssal energy that seeps through the weakened Veil. Brave (or foolhardy) warriors occasionally claim one, gaining immense power — and suffering the inevitable curse.

The kingdoms wage war over territory, resources, and pride. But beneath the surface, a greater threat stirs.

---

## Moraphys: The Inheritors of Morath

**Moraphys** is a secretive NPC faction — the spiritual and literal descendants of the forgemaster Morath. For centuries, they have operated in the shadows, manipulating conflicts between the three kingdoms while slowly preparing for their true goal: **collecting all seven Weapons of Chaos**.

### Moraphys's Plan

Morath discovered (before the Sundering) that if all seven Weapons of Chaos were brought together and unified, they would tear open the Veil permanently — merging Aethermoor with the Abyss. In the resulting chaos, Moraphys believes they can reshape reality itself, becoming gods of a new world.

### Moraphys In-Game Behavior

- Moraphys is an NPC faction. Players cannot join Moraphys.
- Moraphys has a **stronghold** on the world map that grows stronger over the game round.
- Moraphys sends raiding parties to harass players and test defenses.
- Moraphys will attempt to steal Weapons of Chaos from player wielders via NPC attacks.
- As the round progresses, Moraphys grows bolder and stronger — escalating pressure on all players.
- When Moraphys collects **all seven Weapons of Chaos**, the **Endgame Event** triggers.

---

## The Endgame: Gathering of Chaos

When Moraphys possesses all seven Weapons of Chaos:

1. **Announcement**: A world-wide event notification: *"Moraphys has gathered the Weapons of Chaos. The Veil trembles. The world has [X] days."*
2. **Countdown**: A configurable timer begins (TBD: days/weeks). This is the time players have to respond.
3. **Moraphys Stronghold Empowered**: The NPC stronghold becomes vastly more powerful, surrounded by Abyssal energy.
4. **Weapons of Order**: Players must forge Weapons of Order through alliance-level collaboration (see `docs/01-game-design/core-mechanics.md`). These are the only artifacts that can counter the combined Weapons of Chaos.
5. **The Final Battle**: Alliance armies, equipped with Weapons of Order, must assault the Moraphys Stronghold.
6. **Victory**: If Moraphys is defeated, the Weapons of Chaos are destroyed, the Veil is sealed, and the winning alliance is crowned champion of the round.
7. **Defeat**: If the timer expires, or all assaults fail, the Veil shatters. The round ends in **darkness** — no winner. (A narrative consequence, not a punishment — the story continues in the next round.)

## The Prophecy of Order

Ancient texts found in the ruins of the Great Forge speak of a counter to the Weapons of Chaos:

> *"When the seven are gathered and the Veil cries out, only weapons forged in unity — not power, not fear, but the shared will of the free — can silence the Abyss. These are the Weapons of Order, and they can only be born from the forge of cooperation."*

This prophecy is the in-lore justification for why Weapons of Order require **alliance-level collaboration** to craft. No single player, no matter how powerful, can forge them alone.

## Aether & Magic System

- **Aether** is the ambient magical energy of Aethermoor. It flows through the land, concentrated at certain geographic features (forests for Sylvara, oceans for Veridor, mountain peaks for Arkazia).
- **Runes** are crystallized Aether, naturally occurring or artificially refined.
- **Forging** is the process of channeling Aether through runes into metal to create magical weapons.
- The **Veil** is maintained by the balance of Aether. Weapons of Chaos disrupt this balance; Weapons of Order restore it.
