# Kingdom Lore

> Backstory, culture, and motivations of each playable kingdom. Cross-reference with `docs/01-game-design/kingdoms.md` for gameplay stats.

## Veridor — The Tidal Dominion

### Founding

When the Sundering shattered the Aetherial Dominion, the coastal peoples were among the first to adapt. Led by **Admiral Veridus**, a former Council navigator, they retreated to the archipelagos and coastal cities that had survived the cataclysm. Veridus understood that the sea — vast, unpredictable, and resource-rich — was the key to survival.

He unified the scattered coastal settlements under a single flag: the **Tidal Dominion of Veridor**, named after himself — not out of vanity, but because his people insisted. "The man who saved us from the waves deserves to name the shore," they said.

### Culture

Veridorians are merchants, sailors, and pragmatists. They believe that **wealth is power**, and that trade is the most reliable weapon. Their society is structured around **Merchant Houses** — powerful trading families that govern different aspects of Veridor's economy and navy.

Key cultural values:
- **Adaptability**: The sea changes constantly; Veridorians must change with it.
- **Negotiation before combat**: Trade deals are preferred over bloodshed. But when combat is needed, it's swift and decisive.
- **Knowledge of tides**: Veridorians are master cartographers and navigators. They know the world's waterways better than anyone.

### Relationship with the Weapons of Chaos

Veridor possesses extensive records about the Weapons of Chaos from the old Dominion archives salvaged during the Sundering. They view the weapons as **dangerous tools** — useful if wielded correctly, catastrophic if mishandled. Some Merchant Houses secretly fund expeditions to claim Weapons of Chaos, believing they can profit from the power without succumbing to the curse.

**The Tide Ender**, the trident that commands the seas, holds special significance for Veridor. Many Veridorians believe it rightfully belongs to their kingdom.

### Legendary Hero: Admiral Veridus

The founder of Veridor. A brilliant tactician and navigator who charted safe routes through the chaos of the Sundering. His legendary flagship, the **Aethertide**, is said to still sail the deepest waters, crewed by the ghosts of his loyal sailors. Veridorian heroes aspire to his legacy.

### Motivations

- **Control trade routes**: Veridor wants to dominate the world's economy through naval superiority.
- **Claim The Tide Ender**: The Weapon of Chaos most associated with their identity.
- **Survive pragmatically**: Veridor will ally with anyone if the deal is right — and betray anyone if the profit warrants it.

## Sylvara — The Verdant Wilds

### Founding

The jungle peoples survived the Sundering by retreating into the deepest, oldest forests of Aethermoor — places where the trees are so ancient they predate even the Aetherial Dominion. Led by **Elder Sylva**, a former Council lorekeeper and botanist, they discovered that the forests themselves were alive with Aether in ways the Dominion had never fully understood.

Sylva taught her people to **listen to the forest** — to draw power from the living wood, to grow structures rather than build them, and to weave runes from living vines. Under her guidance, the scattered forest tribes united as **Sylvara**, the kingdom of the Verdant Wilds.

### Culture

Sylvarans are druids, rangers, and survivalists. They believe that **nature is the truest forge** — that the greatest weapons are grown, not hammered. Their society is organized into **Groves** — semi-autonomous communities centered around an ancient tree, each governed by a Grove Elder.

Key cultural values:
- **Harmony with nature**: Sylvarans build with the land, not against it. Their structures are living things — treehouses, vine bridges, root-woven walls.
- **Patience and cunning**: The jungle teaches that direct confrontation is wasteful. Ambush, misdirection, and patience are the Sylvaran way.
- **Rune mastery**: Sylvara has the deepest understanding of runes among the three kingdoms. Their Aether sensitivity allows them to perceive and manipulate runes with unmatched skill.

### Relationship with the Weapons of Chaos

Sylvarans fear the Weapons of Chaos more than any other kingdom. They believe the weapons are **wounds in the world** — tears in the Veil that bleed Abyssal energy. Elder Sylva's writings warn that wielding a Weapon of Chaos is akin to "drinking poison to quench thirst."

However, Sylvara's rune mastery gives them unique insight into how the weapons work. Some Grove Elders believe that with enough Primordial Runes, the corrupting influence of a Weapon of Chaos can be temporarily suppressed — a theory that has never been proven.

**Whisper of Thorns**, the bow that never misses, is a source of great debate in Sylvara. Some see it as a natural weapon corrupted; others believe it was always Abyssal.

### Legendary Hero: Elder Sylva

The founder of Sylvara. A brilliant lorekeeper who could hear the "voice of the green" — the Aether flowing through living things. She is revered as a prophet. Her final words — *"The forest remembers what we forget"* — are the kingdom's motto. Sylvaran heroes seek wisdom as much as strength.

### Motivations

- **Protect the Veil**: Sylvara's primary concern is preventing the Veil from shattering. Everything else is secondary.
- **Master rune-craft**: The path to Weapons of Order runs through rune mastery, and Sylvara intends to lead that effort.
- **Survive by stealth**: Sylvara avoids open war when possible, preferring to strike from the shadows and melt back into the forest.

## Arkazia — The Iron Peaks

### Founding

The mountain peoples of Aethermoor were the forge-workers, miners, and warriors of the old Dominion. When the Sundering came, they retreated to the **Iron Peaks** — a range of mountains rich in ore and fortifiable against any assault. Led by **Warlord Arkaz**, a former Council weapons-master and decorated champion of the Dominion's arena, they dug in and built a civilization of stone and steel.

Arkaz believed that the Sundering proved one thing: **only the strong survive**. He restructured his people's society around martial excellence, competitive forging, and an unshakable defensive doctrine. Under his rule, every citizen was a soldier, every smith was revered, and every mountain became a fortress.

### Culture

Arkazians are warriors, smiths, and stoics. They believe that **excellence is earned through struggle** — that comfort breeds weakness. Their society is organized around **Citadels** — fortified mountain cities, each governed by a **Forge-Lord** who is both military commander and master smith.

Key cultural values:
- **Strength through discipline**: Every Arkazian trains in combat from youth. The Colosseum — a gladiatorial arena in every major citadel — is where warriors prove their worth.
- **Honor in craft**: Arkazian smiths are the finest in Aethermoor. To forge a masterwork weapon is considered the highest achievement, equal to winning a great battle.
- **Unbreakable defense**: Arkazians believe that the best offense is making yourself impossible to conquer. Their fortifications are legendary — walls within walls, kill zones, and mountain passes turned into death traps.

### Relationship with the Weapons of Chaos

Arkazians have the most pragmatic view of the Weapons of Chaos. They see them as **flawed masterworks** — incredible feats of forging, corrupted by Morath's arrogance. Many Forge-Lords dream of reforging the Weapons of Chaos to purge the corruption, though none have succeeded.

Arkazia respects the weapons' power but distrusts their influence. Arkazian doctrine states that if you must wield a Weapon of Chaos, you do so in a controlled manner — with handlers, overseers, and a plan to pass it on before the corruption takes hold.

**Gravelord's Fist**, the mountain-shattering war hammer, is considered Arkazia's birthright. Many Forge-Lords have launched expeditions to claim it.

### Legendary Hero: Warlord Arkaz

The founder of Arkazia. A legendarily skilled fighter who won 100 consecutive matches in the Dominion's Grand Arena before the Sundering. He single-handedly held the pass of **Iron Gate** for three days during the civil war, allowing 10,000 refugees to escape to the mountains. The Iron Peaks are named in his honor. Arkazian heroes strive for his combination of martial prowess and selfless duty.

### Motivations

- **Forge supremacy**: Arkazia wants to be the kingdom that crafts the Weapons of Order — cementing their legacy as the world's greatest forgemasters.
- **Claim Gravelord's Fist**: The Weapon of Chaos most associated with their mining and mountain identity.
- **Endure and outlast**: Arkazia's strategy is to build impregnable defenses and let enemies break themselves against the mountains.

## Inter-Kingdom Relations

| Relationship | Description |
|-------------|------------|
| **Veridor ↔ Sylvara** | Cautious trade partners. Veridor wants Sylvara's runes; Sylvara needs Veridor's naval logistics. Trust is limited. |
| **Veridor ↔ Arkazia** | Wary rivals. Veridor's economic power threatens Arkazia's independence. Arkazia's military might threatens Veridor's trade routes. |
| **Sylvara ↔ Arkazia** | Mutual respect, different philosophies. Both value craftsmanship (rune vs. forge), but their methods are fundamentally opposed. Occasional cooperation against Moraphys. |
| **All ↔ Moraphys** | Universal enemy. The one thing that can unite the kingdoms is the threat of Moraphys. But even then, old rivalries and distrust make alliance-building difficult. |
