# Kingdom Roster Descriptions

> Detailed descriptions of all military buildings and troop types for each playable kingdom. For exact troop stats (attack, defense, speed, costs), see `server/internal/config/troops.go` or the generated `client/src/config/generated/troops.json`.
>
> Each kingdom has 5 military buildings (Barracks, Stable, Archery, Workshop, Special) with kingdom-specific display names. Each building trains 3–5 troop types. Total: **140 troops across 7 kingdoms**.

---

## Sylvara

**Roothall (Barracks)** — Sylvara’s infantry lodge, where forest warbands train for close fighting, ambush defense, and shielded woodland warfare.

**Rootguard Spearmen (Barracks)** — disciplined defensive infantry with spears and living-wood shields, built to hold paths and choke points.

**Thornblade Wardens (Barracks)** — sword-and-buckler fighters, quick and precise, used as the reliable core of Sylvaran lines.

**Boarhide Axemen (Barracks)** — tougher shock infantry with heavier axes and hide protection, made to break enemy guards.

**Leafknife Stalkers (Barracks)** — fast skirmishing infantry with paired blades, specialized in flanks and finishing wounded targets.

**Beast Hall (Stable)** — the mount-yard of Sylvara, where riders bond with woodland beasts instead of standard cavalry horses.

**Hart Lancers (Stable)** — swift stag-mounted lancers, elegant and fast, ideal for hit-and-withdraw charges.

**Boar Riders (Stable)** — brutal close-range cavalry on armored boars, used to smash light infantry and disrupt formations.

**Wolf Outriders (Stable)** — fast pursuit riders with javelins, meant for harassment, scouting, and hunting routed enemies.

**Grove Range (Archery)** — Sylvara’s long-range training grounds, blending hunting skill with battlefield marksmanship.

**Longbow Wardens (Archery)** — disciplined long-range archers, the backbone of Sylvara’s missile line.

**Owlshot Hunters (Archery)** — lighter archers built for stealth, precision, and forest skirmishing.

**Thorn Javeliners (Archery)** — mobile throwers with hardened javelins, best at mid-range pressure.

**Stone-sling Foresters (Archery)** — cheap but useful support slingers, strong against lightly armored troops.

**Tree-Sapper Yard (Workshop)** — forest siege workshop using woodcraft, roots, sap, and natural pressure weapons.

**Vine Ballista (Workshop)** — a living-wood bolt launcher used to pin monsters, cavalry, or siege crews.

**Log Trebuchet (Workshop)** — Sylvara’s heavy stone-thrower, built from enormous trunk frames.

**Root Ram (Workshop)** — a reinforced siege ram covered in bark and root plating.

**Spore-Pot Thrower (Workshop)** — a specialized engine that hurls toxic or blinding spore vessels.

**Spirit Glade (Special)** — sacred special building for Sylvara’s rare bonded, mystical, and druidic elites.

**Beastmasters (Special)** — handlers who fight beside sacred beasts and enhance nearby animal units.

**Shapeshifter Scouts (Special)** — elusive elites who blur the line between warrior and forest predator.

**Life Healers (Special)** — battlefield sustain units using restorative nature power instead of pure offense.

**Antler Seers (Special)** — support mystics who guide, bless, and disrupt through omen and ritual.

## Arkazia

**Red Bastion (Barracks)** — Arkazia’s infantry stronghold, focused on fortress discipline, shield warfare, and mountain-line defense.

**Shield Guard (Barracks)** — core sword-and-kite infantry, dependable, defensive, and visually iconic for Arkazia.

**Pike Guard (Barracks)** — anti-cavalry formation troops trained to hold approaches and punish charges.

**Stonebreaker (Barracks)** — heavy shock infantry with massive hammers, built to crush armor and break frontlines.

**Rampart Halberdiers (Barracks)** — polearm specialists for anti-armor fighting, line support, and wall defense.

**Arknight Stables (Stable)** — the cavalry yard of Arkazia, where disciplined riders train for fortress support and decisive charges.

**Arknight Lancers (Stable)** — elite lance cavalry, the classic heavy striking force of the kingdom.

**Redcrest Cavaliers (Stable)** — mace-and-shield riders for disciplined melee cavalry engagements.

**Banner Riders (Stable)** — prestige cavalry who carry command banners and improve allied morale.

**Ravine Pursuers (Stable)** — lighter horsemen used for pursuit, skirmishing, and flanking in rough terrain.

**Ridge Range (Archery)** — missile training for mountain and fortress warfare, favoring reliable ranged support over ornate archery.

**Hill Slingers (Archery)** — practical missile troops trained for steep terrain and defensive bombardment.

**Ridge Crossbowmen (Archery)** — armor-piercing ranged infantry designed for wall and choke-point fighting.

**Javelin Climbers (Archery)** — mobile throwers suited to broken ground and close support.

**Crag Bowmen (Archery)** — disciplined war-bow troops with better range than the lighter missile corps.

**Stonecaller Yard (Workshop)** — Arkazia’s siege and engineering yard, built around stone, ramps, walls, and heavy siege utility.

**Mountain Trebuchet (Workshop)** — a fortress-grade heavy artillery engine for long-range bombardment.

**Ram Engineers (Workshop)** — armored crews who build and escort gate-breaking siege rams.

**Mantlet Pushers (Workshop)** — shielded advance crews bringing cover to infantry and ranged troops.

**Bridgewright Crew (Workshop)** — tactical engineers able to create assault paths, crossings, and siege access.

**Chapter Fortress (Special)** — Arkazia’s special order hall, where elite sworn units, commanders, and prestige warriors are raised.

**Banner Knights (Special)** — knightly infantry or mounted elites who fight as mobile standards of the realm.

**Oathsworn Champions (Special)** — elite duel-capable warriors trusted for hard breaches and last stands.

**Bastion Marshals (Special)** — heavily protected command fighters who anchor lines and inspire nearby troops.

**Arknight Captains (Special)** — rare cavalry leaders representing Arkazia’s highest mounted tradition.

**Road Legionaries (Barracks)** — sword-and-kite infantry, orderly and professional, suited to roads, ports, and line battles.

**Harbor Pike (Barracks)** — disciplined spear-wall infantry used to defend wharves, docks, and narrow approaches.

**Courier Riders (Stable)** — light cavalry scouts focused on speed, messages, and harassment.

**Chart Range (Archery)** — ranged corps blending port defense, ship warfare, and inland marksman traditions.

**Highland Longbowmen (Archery)** — longer-range archers recruited from Veridor’s tougher upland regions.
